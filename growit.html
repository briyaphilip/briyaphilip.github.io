Grow It!

Overview

Grow It!'s goal is to help families impacted by food insecurity by increasing their accessibility to sustainable and nutritious food. We chose to create this product based on the following sustainable development goals: zero hunger and responsible consumption and production. From our research, we learned that some of the main factors leading to food insecurity are poverty, unemployment, and geography. Grow It! addressed this issue by providing detailed instructions on how to grow plants at home, how to reduce waste, and how to consume responsibly. Grow It! also offers personalized plant suggestions, community support, and access to seeds through a local seed library.

How are we addressing this issue?

Grow It! provides users detailed instructions on how to grow plants, reduce waste, and comsume responsibly. Grow It! has guides on all of these topics so that users have endless information on how to grow their own food sustainably and easily.


How is it different?

Grow It! provides users personalized plant suggestions based on their available space and the climate in their area. Grow It! also provides partnership with local farmers and seed libraries so that users do not have to go out and find seeds on their own. Direct virtual support is provided if users have any questions or problems growing their food. Lastly, a community support page is provided to lessen the isolating feelings that come along with food insecurity. It is important for our users to not feel alone in this journey.

Community Page:



The community page is a page of the website that I created. On this page, users can post about their plant journey, any problems that they have faced when growing plants, and also general comments about how Grow It! has helped them personally. Users can also comment on other posts to share knowledge and to connect with others. We decided to include a community page on the website to create a sense of togetherness for those facing food insecurity. Food insecurity can be such an isolating experience, so the community page aims to create a support system for those experiencing this issue.

Evaluation of Designs: 

As we designed Grow It!, accessibility was at the top of our minds. We made sure that all of the color contrasts were AAA accessible so that the text is readable. Our team also did a Heuristic evaluation to find any possible usablity problems to solve throughout our iterative design process.
